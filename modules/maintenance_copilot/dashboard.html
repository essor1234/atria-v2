AI Maintenance Knowledge Copilot

P1 · Concept Brief & Brainstorm
Prepared for the Maintenance Control Center (MCC) and Engineering Teams · July 1, 2026
Human-in-the-loop, always. The copilot combines RAG, document intelligence, and knowledge graphs to retrieve, validate, and cross-reference maintenance documentation faster and with fewer errors — but a licensed engineer stays in the loop for every dispatch decision. Dispatch is never fully automated.

Problem statement

MCC and engineering teams must continuously reference large volumes of documentation. As fleets grow and operational pressure increases, manual validation becomes more time-consuming and more susceptible to human error.

AMMMELCDL TSMEngineering orders Historical defect records

Current challenges

Search burden

Significant time spent searching and cross-referencing across multiple manuals.

Reference errors

Incorrect AMM task or MEL item references may cause delays or operational risk.

Manual, inconsistent entry

Defect classifications and dispatch conditions are hand-entered and inconsistent.

Fragmented knowledge

Institutional knowledge is scattered with no unified way to query it.

Human factors

Fatigue and workload raise the chance of missed information — worst during AOG.

Brainstormed use cases · Pilot maps to stated pilot scope

Retrieval & search

  • Natural-language retrieval of AMM/TSM procedures from a defect description or ATA chapter.Pilot
  • Voice / free-text query for MCC during live AOG, returning the top procedure with page/section citation.
  • Unified search across AMM, MEL, CDL, TSM, and engineering orders in one query.
  • Automatic surfacing of the correct manual revision — never a superseded page.

Validation & cross-referencing

  • Validate defect entries against approved documentation before submission.Pilot
  • Recommend the relevant AMM/MEL/CDL/TSM reference for a defect write-up.Pilot
  • Highlight inconsistencies (e.g. dispatch condition vs cited MEL item) before sign-off.Pilot
  • Cross-check classification against historical similar defects.
  • Detect missing placarding, tooling, or interval requirements implied by a MEL/CDL item.

Decision support

  • Dispatch-readiness summary: open items, applicable MEL/CDL relief, time-limited deadlines.
  • Alerting on approaching MEL category deadlines (A/B/C/D).
  • Suggested next TSM troubleshooting step from symptoms + prior fix history.
  • Fleet-wide pattern detection: recurring defects across tail numbers.

Knowledge capture & explainability

  • Explainable reasoning with source references for every recommendation.Pilot
  • Confidence scoring; low-confidence results routed for mandatory manual review.
  • Feedback loop: engineer corrections improve retrieval ranking and flag doc gaps.
  • OCR / digitization of legacy paper logbooks and historical defect records.

Compliance & audit

  • Full audit trail linking every AI-suggested reference to document, revision, and page.
  • Mandatory human-in-the-loop sign-off — the copilot recommends, a licensed engineer decides and signs.
  • Version-aware indexing reflecting the currently approved AMM/MEL/CDL revision.
